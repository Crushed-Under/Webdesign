Huge chunks of major rainforest are being chewed up

The ecological impacts of deforestation are obvious with destruction of local flora and local fauna habitat reduced in size. Hundreds of square kilometers of tropical rainforests are removed daily from all over the world. Huge chunks of forests are carved away constantly in places like the amazon rainforest, the jungles in sub-saharan africa and all over south-eastern asia. This loss of forest has larger impacts, not only from what is immediately obvious for example plant life absorbs carbon dioxide from the atmosphere helping to combat the greenhouse gas effect. the massive reduction in the amount of forests on the planet only further hinders the planet's ability to slow down the process of global warming.
